The Tesla Model S is an all-electric luxury sedan made by Tesla, Inc. It was first introduced in 2012, and has been updated several times since then. The current Model S has a range of up to 402 miles on a single charge, making it one of the longest-range electric cars on the market. It is also known for its performance, with some models able to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in as little as 2.3 seconds. In addition to its impressive speed and range, the Model S also features a sleek and modern design, as well as a range of advanced technology and safety features.

The Tesla Model Y is an all-electric crossover SUV that was first introduced in March 2019. It is built on the same platform as the Model 3 sedan, but it has a taller, more spacious body and can seat up to seven passengers. The Model Y is available in several different versions, including a long-range model that can go up to 315 miles on a single charge and a performance model that can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 3.5 seconds. It also features a number of advanced driver-assistant features, such as Autopilot and Full Self-Driving (FSD) capabilities.

Tesla Full Self-Driving (FSD) is a suite of advanced driver-assistance system (ADAS) features that are designed to make Tesla vehicles capable of fully autonomous driving. The FSD package includes a number of features that enable a Tesla vehicle to navigate roads and traffic without any input from the driver, including radar, cameras, and ultrasonic sensors that provide the vehicle with 360-degree awareness of its surroundings.
